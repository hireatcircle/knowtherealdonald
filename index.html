Know The Real Donald Trump

A glimpse into the life of the 45th & 47th President of the United States

Donald John Trump was born on June 14, 1946, in Queens, New York. He is a businessman, television personality, and the 45th President of the United States. Trump is known for his outspoken style, his business acumen, and his successful, albeit controversial, presidency.

Trump's early career began in the family business, the Trump Organization, where he built a portfolio of real estate holdings. He expanded the business significantly, taking it to new heights and becoming one of the most well-known figures in American business.

In 2016, Trump ran as the Republican candidate for President of the United States and won against Democratic nominee Hillary Clinton. His presidency focused on issues like immigration reform, tax cuts, deregulation, and a "America First" foreign policy.

In the 2020 election, Trump was defeated by Joe Biden, but he continued to exert significant influence in the political landscape. As of now, he is preparing for his potential return in the 2024 elections as a candidate for the 47th President of the United States.

Trump's legacy is defined by his unorthodox approach to politics, his polarizing public persona, and his continued involvement in the political sphere.

Donald Trump's Life and Career Timeline

1946

Born in Queens, New York

Donald John Trump was born on June 14, 1946, in Queens, New York, to Fred and Mary Trump.

1971

Becomes President of Trump Organization

Donald Trump took over his father’s real estate and construction business, renaming it the Trump Organization. He expanded its scope into Manhattan.

1980s

Building an Empire

During the 1980s, Donald Trump built several iconic properties, including Trump Tower in Manhattan and other notable real estate developments, solidifying his name in the business world.

2004

The Apprentice Premieres

Trump became a television personality, hosting the reality TV show The Apprentice. His catchphrase "You're fired!" became widely recognized.

2015

Announces Presidential Candidacy

On June 16, 2015, Donald Trump announced his candidacy for the President of the United States as a Republican candidate, marking the beginning of his political career.

2016

Wins the 2016 Presidential Election

Donald Trump won the U.S. presidential election, defeating Democratic nominee Hillary Clinton. He became the 45th president of the United States.

2017

Inauguration as 45th President

Trump was inaugurated as the 45th president of the United States on January 20, 2017. His presidency began with a focus on economic policies, immigration reform, and tax cuts.

2020

COVID-19 Pandemic Response

During his presidency, Trump oversaw the U.S. response to the COVID-19 pandemic, which included major economic relief packages and controversial handling of public health advice.

2020

2020 Presidential Election

Donald Trump ran for re-election in 2020 but lost to Joe Biden in a closely contested race. He contested the results, claiming voter fraud, though no substantial evidence was found.

2024

Elected 47th President of the United States

Donald Trump wins the election in 2024 and is set to serve as the 47th president of the United States, marking a historic return to office after his first term from 2017 to 2021.

2024

Second Presidential Run

After losing the 2020 election, Donald Trump announced his second bid for the presidency, aiming to return to power and continue his political vision for the country.
